Homemade Sloppy Joes

Homemade Sloppy Joes don't have to be difficult to eat. Despite the name of this iconic
retro dish, the secret to a great Sloppy Joe is a thick, rich, almost dry consistency, which
allows the sandwich to be eaten with your hands. Serve on hamburger buns.


Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds extra-lean ground beef
  • ½ onion, diced
  • 1 green pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¾ cup ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt, or to taste
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 dash Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper, or to taste

Directions

Step 1
Place ground beef and onion in a large skillet over medium heat; cook and stir until beef is browned
and crumbled, about 10 minutes.

Step 2
Stir in green pepper and garlic; cook and stir until softened, 2 to 3 minutes. Add 1 cup water and stir,
scraping the pan to dissolve any brown flavor bits from the bottom of the skillet.

Step 3
Stir in ketchup, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, salt, black pepper, and Worcestershire sauce. Add remaining
1 cup water and bring mixture to a boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally, until liquid has
evaporated and mixture is thick, about 40 minutes

Step 4
Season with salt, black pepper, Worcestershire sauce, and cayenne pepper.
